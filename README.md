## Abstract
The energy crisis in 2022 led to a surge in natural gas prices, which has in turn caused a stabilised inflation response with a lagged peak of a couple of months in the UK. A standard New Keynesian model with exogenously priced energy as a production input fails to replicate this hump-shaped and persistent response. I propose three extension to the baseline model to replicate the observed inflation dynamics in the UK: a vertically integrated production network where upstream industry firms use energy and labour to produce intermediate goods while downstream firms use intermediate goods and labour to produce consumption goods; myopia in downstream firms’ pricing decisions reflecting on the difficulty or reluctance of forecasting future prices of intermediate goods; real wage rigidities as a parsimonious way to represent labour market frictions. Simulation exercise indicates that all three extensions are essential in generating a hump-shaped and persistent inflation response to an exogenous energy price shock, and a lack of any of the three fails the replication of inflation dynamics.
